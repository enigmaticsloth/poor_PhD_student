About Me

I am a bioengineering PhD student facing significant financial challenges. My stipend barely covers my basic living expenses, making it difficult to afford meals, maintain savings, and pay rent. The financial strain often forces me to work long hours and stay up late to keep up with my research. Despite these hardships, I am dedicated to completing my degree and contributing valuable insights to the field of biomedical science.

Research Focus

My research focuses on lung-on-a-chip technology, which integrates organ-on-a-chip systems with stem cells to study lung development and regeneration. This innovative approach aims to develop new therapeutic strategies for lung adenocarcinoma, a type of lung cancer.

Bioreactor in Research

In addition to lung-on-a-chip technology, we utilize a bioreactor to culture human lung tissues. This setup allows us to create a controlled environment that mimics the physiological conditions of the human body, facilitating the study of lung development and regeneration. The bioreactor enables precise manipulation of variables such as oxygen levels, nutrient supply, and mechanical forces, which are crucial for understanding the complex processes involved in lung tissue formation and healing.

Significance of Research

The development of a lung-on-a-chip model provides a more accurate platform for studying lung diseases by mimicking the physiological environment of the human lung. This technology not only advances our understanding of lung injuries and diseases but also reduces the need for animal testing and lowers the costs associated with clinical trials. Consequently, it accelerates the development of safer and more effective treatments.
